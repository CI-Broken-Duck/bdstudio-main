# Thailand SMART Visa “S”: A Complete Guide for Foreign Startup Founders

Thailand is rapidly trying to position itself as a destination for innovation and high-tech entrepreneurship. One of the tools in its arsenal is the **SMART Visa program**, which offers foreign founders a pathway to live and build in Thailand without the friction of traditional visa and work permit systems.

This article is a **comprehensive, founder-focused guide** to the **SMART Visa “S” stream**—tailored specifically for entrepreneurs planning to launch or grow a tech startup in Thailand. We'll break down:

- What types of startups qualify
- How to find and structure a Thai partnership
- How to incorporate a business locally
- The documents you’ll need to prepare, including a business plan, pitch deck, and live demo
- Health insurance requirements, common pitfalls, and renewal timelines
- Alternative visas for founders who aren’t ready to apply

Whether you're bootstrapping an AI product or joining an accelerator in Chiang Mai or Bangkok, this guide will walk you through every major step with practical, real-world context.

> This is not a theoretical overview—it's a founder’s manual for using Thailand as a strategic base to build, iterate, and extend your runway.

Let’s begin.

## 1. What Is the SMART Visa “S”? (~600 words)

Thailand’s **SMART Visa** program, launched in 2018, is a targeted initiative by the Board of Investment (BOI) designed to attract foreign professionals, investors, executives, and startup founders into the country’s high-tech and innovative sectors—known as the “S‑Curve” industries (digital tech, automation, biotech, AI, robotics, biotech, etc.). Unlike bulk immigration categories, SMART is tailored to promote deep economic involvement and local value creation in priority industries :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}.

### Why the “S” Stream Exists

Within the SMART system, the **“S” stream** is solely for **startup entrepreneurs** who have already established a company in Thailand. As of early 2025 reforms, SMART now supports only one streamlined pathway for founders: a **2-year renewable visa**. Earlier 6-month and 1-year options have been discontinued to focus the program on companies that are operational and scaling :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}.

This pivot represents Thailand’s shift toward long-term retention. Applicants must demonstrate tangible business progress (already incorporated and operating) rather than just intent to launch. Eligibility requires:

- A **Thai-registered startup** in an eligible S‑Curve industry
- Founder holds **≥ 25% ownership or serves as a company director**
- Maintains a **฿600,000 personal savings minimum**, held for at least three months
- Valid **health insurance** covering the visa duration :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Upon approval, the visa grants:

- **2-year stay**, renewable
- **Work permit exemption** for work within the registered company
- **Annual reporting** (instead of every 90 days)
- **Multiple entry** without re-entry permit
- Dependent visas for spouse and children (no separate work permit required) :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

### How It Differs from Other Visa Options

- **Tourist visas** (e.g., TR, OA) prohibit any work or company formation.  
- **Non‑B business visas** require work permits and mandate employing Thai nationals (e.g., 4:1 quota for foreigners).  
- **Elite/LTR visas** provide long-term residence but **do not allow work or business operations**.  
In contrast, SMART-S offers **legitimate operational freedom** to build and manage a startup.

### Startup-Focused by Design

SMART‑S is not for consultants, freelancers, or passive investors. The aim is to bring **active founder-operators** who are legally and financially accountable for a Thai-registered tech venture. This aligns with the government’s goal to **accelerate innovation, create high-value jobs, and push Thailand toward Industry 4.0**.

### Why SMART‑S Matters Right Now

- **Legal clarity**: replace burdensome single-entry visas with a valid bridge to startup operations.  
- **Operational simplicity**: no requirement for a work permit, Thai hires quota, or BOI incentives to start building.  
- **Stability**: clear path for visa-dependent founders and their families.  
If you plan to build a scalable, legally compliant tech business in Thailand, SMART‑S offers the **only direct and structured support**—critical to staying operational and competitive in 2025 and beyond.

## 2. Who Should Apply – What Startups Qualify (~500 words)

The SMART Visa “S” is not for every entrepreneur. It is designed specifically for **tech-driven startups** operating within Thailand’s targeted future-facing industries. If you’re running a conventional business—like a dropshipping store or a digital agency—you are unlikely to qualify. This program exists to attract foreign founders who are **building innovative products with scalable potential** in industries the Thai government deems critical to long-term economic transformation.

### ✅ Qualifying Startups

To be considered eligible under the SMART “S” visa, your company must:

- **Operate in a targeted industry** (also known as “S-Curve” or “Next-Gen” sectors)
- Be **registered and incorporated in Thailand**
- Have a **core offering that is technology-based or innovation-led**
- Show progress beyond an idea—e.g., MVP, users, or R&D outputs

These sectors include (but are not limited to):

- **Digital Technology**: SaaS, AI/ML platforms, cybersecurity, fintech, e-commerce infrastructure  
- **Automation and Robotics**: IoT systems, industrial robotics, process automation  
- **Biotech and Agritech**: vertical farming, gene-based diagnostics, synthetic biology  
- **Future Food**: alternative protein, food innovation using AI or science-based processes  
- **Biofuels and Biochemicals**: next-gen materials, green energy innovation  
- **Medical Technology**: healthtech apps, medical devices, bioinformatics platforms  
- **Aerospace and Aviation**: drone technologies, aerospace software, logistics platforms

Startups in these spaces that use **IP, proprietary tech, or algorithmic models** are favored. If your product or service has **some form of defensible innovation or scalable platform logic**, you’re likely a fit.

### ❌ Non-Qualifying Startups

SMART Visa “S” is **not** a backdoor visa route for lifestyle entrepreneurs or freelancers. Here are examples that typically do *not* qualify:

- A Shopify store reselling third-party goods  
- A freelance content or design agency  
- A crypto investment fund with no Thai entity or product  
- A real estate company or lead-generation service  
- A mobile app clone with no IP or novel features

The line is drawn at **originality, innovation, and sector alignment**. The startup must show how it adds value to Thailand’s economic strategy—particularly through R&D, digital transformation, or global competitiveness.

### Red Flags That Undermine Eligibility

- No tech team or technical founder  
- No MVP or product in development  
- Operating in a saturated, non-innovative space  
- No incorporation in Thailand or attempt to avoid formal setup  
- Using a nominee structure to bypass ownership restrictions

### Practical Rule of Thumb

If your business could qualify for **BOI promotion in a tech category**, it likely aligns with the SMART Visa too. The SMART “S” program functions as a **founder-first alternative** to BOI with fewer local hire quotas and faster access to a work-legal visa.

### Closing Note

SMART “S” is **not a passive visa**—it’s a build visa. You must be ready to actively grow a Thai-based business in a sector that matters to the national agenda. If that’s you, and your startup demonstrates tech-forward thinking, you’re the exact kind of founder this program wants to attract.

## 3. SMART Visa Benefits (~400 words)

The SMART Visa “S” offers several advantages that make it one of the most founder-friendly immigration pathways for building a startup in Thailand. Unlike traditional business visas or long-stay options like the LTR (Long-Term Resident) visa, the SMART “S” is **designed specifically for foreign entrepreneurs launching a Thai-registered tech business**.

Here are the primary benefits:

### ✅ No Work Permit Required

Founders under the SMART “S” visa can legally operate and manage their Thai startup **without needing a separate work permit**. This removes one of the biggest hurdles of the traditional B visa system, which normally requires:

- A company with 2M THB registered capital per foreigner
- At least 4 Thai employees per foreign employee
- Extensive paperwork and approvals from the Ministry of Labour

By contrast, the SMART Visa streamlines this entirely—you are **pre-cleared to work under the visa itself**, so long as you're working within your own certified startup.

### ✅ Visa Validity Up to 2 Years

As of 2025, the SMART “S” visa is issued as a **2-year renewable multiple-entry visa**. This provides significantly more stability than:

- A tourist visa (60–90 days max)
- A business visa (typically 1 year, with work permit constraints)
- A non-immigrant B visa, which requires quarterly immigration reporting and corporate sponsorship

Founders can focus on building product and team without constantly cycling through short-term visa renewals.

### ✅ Family Inclusion and Work Rights

Spouses and dependents (children under 20) can be included in the SMART “S” visa application. They receive their own “SMART O” visa, which:

- Allows them to **reside in Thailand for the same duration** as the principal holder
- **Grants spouses the right to work** in Thailand without a separate work permit
- Simplifies school registration, healthcare, and general family mobility

This makes it viable for long-term relocation and stability as a founder with a partner or children.

### ✅ Reduced Immigration Reporting

Unlike most visa types which require a **90-day check-in** with immigration, SMART Visa holders only need to **report once per year**. This removes a significant layer of administrative burden.

### ✅ Comparison to Business and LTR Visas

| Feature                        | SMART “S” Visa           | Business Visa (Non-B)      | LTR Visa                        |
|-------------------------------|--------------------------|-----------------------------|---------------------------------|
| Work permit required?         | No                       | Yes                         | Yes (with conditions)           |
| Max validity                  | 2 years (renewable)      | 1 year (typical)            | 10 years (5+5, renews)          |
| Thai company needed?          | Yes (startup must exist) | Yes                         | Not necessarily                 |
| For building startups?        | Yes (specifically)       | No (general business scope) | Not startup-focused             |
| Family inclusion              | Yes, with work rights    | Limited                     | Yes, with various benefits      |

Overall, the SMART Visa “S” is **purpose-built for founders**. It simplifies legal work status, improves long-term planning, and aligns with startup-specific goals—not just passive residence or investment models.

## 4. Step-by-Step Application Process (~700 words)

Applying for the SMART Visa “S” as a foreign startup founder in Thailand is a multi-phase process that involves both digital submission and in-person follow-through. Below is a detailed guide outlining every major step, from account creation to final visa issuance.

### Step 1: Create an Account on the SMART Visa Portal

Start by registering on the official SMART Visa website managed by Thailand’s Board of Investment (BOI):

- **Website**: [https://smart-visa.boi.go.th](https://smart-visa.boi.go.th)
- Use your personal email address and set a secure password
- Select “SMART S” as your visa type
- The portal dashboard will allow you to start a new application and monitor progress

**Tip**: Be prepared to use a desktop or laptop computer—mobile interfaces are not fully supported.

---

### Step 2: Upload Required Documents as PDFs

Once your account is active, you’ll be asked to upload PDF versions of the required documents. These must be clearly labeled and in English or Thai. Common document requirements include:

- **Passport bio page** (valid at least 6 months with 2+ blank pages)
- **Personal photo** (white background, passport size, taken within the last 6 months)
- **Company registration documents** (e.g., DBD registration, shareholder structure)
- **Proof of capital** (600,000 THB held for 3+ months; 180,000 THB per family member)
- **Health insurance** coverage for your full stay (including family, if applicable)
- **Pitch deck and business plan** (more on this in later sections)
- **Startup endorsement letter** (if applicable)

Files must not exceed 10MB each. Name your files with clear identifiers (e.g., `passport_craig_smith.pdf`, `health_insurance_family.pdf`).

---

### Step 3: Application Review and Government Endorsement

After submission, your application enters the review and endorsement phase. This is handled by one of several Thai government bodies depending on your sector:

- **Board of Investment (BOI)**
- **Digital Economy Promotion Agency (DEPA)**
- **National Innovation Agency (NIA)**

As of 2025, **formal pre-endorsement is no longer strictly required**, but these agencies may still be involved in reviewing your submitted pitch deck, financials, or market plan.

Your business must fall within one of Thailand’s targeted industries and demonstrate innovation, scalability, and technical merit. Agencies may request:

- Proof of incorporation or traction (e.g., customer data, pilot programs)
- Specifics of your product or technology stack
- Timeline and employment plans for Thai staff

This phase typically takes **30 working days** but may vary based on backlog and industry relevance.

---

### Step 4: Receive Letter of Qualification Endorsement

Once your startup and personal qualifications are verified, the SMART Visa committee will issue a **Letter of Qualification Endorsement**. This is your formal approval to apply for the visa itself.

You will receive this letter via email and through the SMART Visa portal. Print multiple copies—you’ll need it for your next appointments.

---

### Step 5: Book Your Immigration Appointment

With your endorsement letter in hand, proceed to schedule an appointment for visa affixation. You can do this at:

- **One Stop Service Center (OSS)** in Bangkok  
  *Chamchuri Square Building, Floor 18*
- **EEC Labour Administration Center** in Chonburi (if your business is EEC-based)
- **Royal Thai Consulate or Embassy** (if applying from abroad)

You must bring:

- Passport  
- Printed endorsement letter  
- Completed TM.6 and STM.6 forms  
- Photo (passport size)  
- Any additional supporting documents requested in your confirmation email

Visa fee: **10,000 THB per year** (payable on-site)

Appointments typically take **30–60 minutes**, and visa stickers are affixed the same day or within 1–2 business days.

---

### Step 6: Report Arrival and Finalize Rights

Once your SMART Visa is affixed:

1. If entering Thailand for the first time, notify BOI of your arrival by emailing a copy of your **arrival stamp and visa sticker** to: `smartvisa@boi.go.th`
2. BOI will issue your **Notification Letter of Rights for SMART Visa Holders**, confirming work and stay privileges
3. Family members (SMART “O” holders) must also be registered for work rights (if applicable)

---

### Estimated Timeline Summary

| Step | Description | Typical Time |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| Account registration | SMART Visa portal | Immediate |
| Document upload | PDF submissions | 1–2 days |
| Government review | BOI/DEPA/NIA assessment | ~30 working days |
| Endorsement issuance | Qualification letter | 2–3 days |
| Immigration appointment | Visa affixation | 1–2 days |
| Total | End-to-end duration | ~6 weeks |

---

### Common Delays and Issues

- **Missing or mislabeled documents** can reset your application clock
- Health insurance policies not clearly showing coverage period are often rejected
- Visa affixation delays occur around Thai holidays or budget year-end (September)
- Applying from abroad may add an extra **7–10 days** due to embassy coordination

If possible, initiate your SMART Visa “S” application **at least 2 months in advance** of your target entry date into Thailand.

## 5. Finding a Thai Partner (~600 words)

For most foreign startup founders applying for the SMART Visa “S,” one unavoidable step is forming a Thai legal entity—and that means finding a Thai partner. Unlike countries such as Singapore or the U.S., where foreigners can own and operate companies independently, Thai corporate law restricts foreign ownership in most non-promoted sectors to **49%**. As a result, a **Thai majority shareholder (51%) is typically required**, unless your company qualifies for a BOI promotion or uses other structuring exemptions (which are rare and not guaranteed for early-stage startups).

### Why a Thai Partner Is Functionally Required

To submit a valid SMART Visa application under the “S” stream, applicants must demonstrate:

- A **registered Thai company** in a targeted industry
- Thai-based incorporation with DBD registration
- Capitalization and bank balances meeting minimum thresholds

Without a Thai-registered company, you cannot fulfill the core eligibility requirements—even if your product, team, or traction is impressive. This makes the Thai partner issue not just a business decision, but a **structural necessity** for any foreigner pursuing the SMART Visa track.

---

### Co-Founder vs. Nominee: Understanding the Options

Foreign founders often explore two main routes:

**1. Real Thai Co-Founder:**  
In this model, the Thai partner is an active part of the business—contributing to product, operations, or market strategy. This setup works well if:
- The Thai partner brings technical or market expertise
- Both parties agree on equity splits, governance, and long-term roles
- Proper legal and shareholder documentation is in place

**2. Passive Nominee Shareholder:**  
A common (but risky) alternative is to register the company using a Thai national as a “nominee” shareholder. This person holds shares on paper but is not involved in operations. This structure:
- Violates the spirit (and often the letter) of Thailand’s Foreign Business Act
- Can be legally challenged, especially during audits or fundraising
- Poses major trust and control risks, particularly with bank accounts and DBD registration

If detected, nominee structures can lead to **visa cancellation**, **deportation**, or loss of corporate rights. They should be approached with extreme caution, if at all.

---

### Formalizing the Partnership: Agreements and Equity

Regardless of which route you take, clear and legally binding agreements are essential:

- **Shareholder Agreement:** Defines roles, voting rights, share transfers, and exit conditions
- **Power of Attorney / Voting Proxy:** Gives you control over business decisions despite minority equity
- **Board Resolution Templates:** Pre-agree on decision-making protocols for major business moves
- **Equity Vesting:** Especially critical if your Thai partner is a co-founder rather than a nominee

Thai civil and commercial law governs most of these agreements. It’s strongly recommended to work with a **Thai corporate lawyer** to ensure enforceability and clarity, particularly in bilingual contracts.

---

### Cultural and Trust Considerations

More than legal or financial risk, the **human dimension** is where most foreign founders struggle. Trust is not easily transacted in Thailand—especially across language, legal, and cultural barriers. Issues to watch for:

- **Misaligned expectations:** Thai partners may view the business as a side project or low-risk play
- **Indirect communication:** Avoid assumptions; confirm understanding on timelines, costs, and responsibilities
- **Risk aversion:** Thai business culture can be more conservative and consensus-driven than many Western founders expect
- **Reputation stakes:** A wrong choice in partner can lead to loss of corporate control, banking issues, or damage to your startup’s credibility

One of the most common long-term problems for foreign founders is realizing too late that their Thai partner has different values, goals, or risk tolerance. 

If you’re serious about building in Thailand, spend **significant upfront time vetting your partner**. That means in-person meetings, trial collaborations, and deep mutual understanding—not just formal compliance.

---

### Summary

Finding a Thai partner is not just a checkbox in the SMART Visa process—it’s one of the most strategically important decisions you’ll make as a foreign entrepreneur in Thailand. You need a partner who understands the vision, is aligned on governance, and can be trusted in both legal and interpersonal matters. Whether you're forming a real co-founding team or structuring for compliance, invest in professional guidance and build trust carefully.


## 6. Incorporating Your Business in Thailand (~800 words)

Incorporating a Thai company is a mandatory step for foreign founders applying under the SMART Visa “S” stream. This legal entity serves as the foundation for your visa application—and your startup’s operations inside the country. However, setting up a company in Thailand is more complex than in many startup-friendly jurisdictions, and understanding the local rules, processes, and timelines is essential.

This section breaks down the process step-by-step, covering Department of Business Development (DBD) registration, director requirements, shareholding rules, tax registration, banking, and the key distinctions between BOI-promoted and non-BOI companies.

---

### Step 1: Company Name Reservation with DBD

The process begins with reserving your company name with Thailand’s Department of Business Development (DBD):

- Visit the DBD e-registration portal: [https://ereg.dbd.go.th/](https://ereg.dbd.go.th/)
- Submit three name choices in order of preference
- Names must be unique and comply with Thai naming conventions (no royal or sensitive terms)
- Reservation is usually approved within 1–2 business days and is valid for 30 days

You can complete this process yourself or through a lawyer or incorporation service.

---

### Step 2: Prepare Company Documents

The following key documents are needed for company registration:

- **Memorandum of Association (MOA):** Lists company name, objectives, registered address, shareholder names, and capital
- **Articles of Association (Bylaws):** Defines company governance and internal rules
- **List of Shareholders and Directors**
- **Affidavit and company form registration (Form Bor. Aor. Jor. 1)**
- **Company Objectives:** Select from DBD’s standardized list or define custom goals relevant to your startup

At this stage, you’ll also determine:

- **Registered Capital** (typically 1–2 million THB minimum for SMART Visa applicants)
- **Shareholding Structure** (a Thai majority, unless BOI promoted or exempted)
- **Company Address** (must be a commercial address, not a residential condo)

Legal assistance is strongly recommended at this step to ensure your company documents are complete and compliant.

---

### Step 3: Register the Company

Once your documents are ready, you register the company either online or in-person at the DBD office:

- Pay the government registration fee (~5,500 THB on average)
- Submit signed documents and shareholder IDs
- A **Thai national ID and house registration (Tabien Baan)** are required for all Thai shareholders and directors
- Foreign shareholders must provide passport copies and visa status (if present in Thailand)

Registration can be completed within 3–5 business days if documents are in order.

---

### Step 4: Tax ID and VAT Registration

After company formation, you must obtain:

- **Tax ID (PIN) number** from the Revenue Department within 60 days
- **VAT registration** (if you expect to earn over 1.8 million THB annually or need to import/export)

These registrations allow your startup to issue tax invoices, pay corporate income tax, and operate legally.

---

### Step 5: Open a Thai Company Bank Account

This is one of the most difficult steps for foreign founders due to banking compliance and KYC:

- Major banks include Bangkok Bank, Kasikornbank, Siam Commercial Bank
- Requirements vary, but most require:
  - Company affidavit
  - Tax ID certificate
  - Company seal
  - All directors present in person
  - Proof of address and lease agreement
- Non-Thai directors may also need to show valid visas or entry stamps

This step can take up to 2–3 weeks and may require local assistance.

---

### BOI Promotion vs. Standard Incorporation

There are two tracks for company formation:

**1. Standard Thai Limited Company:**
- 51% Thai ownership required unless exempted
- Subject to Foreign Business Act (FBA) restrictions
- Requires 4 Thai employees per foreign work permit (not required under SMART Visa)
- Cannot engage in restricted sectors unless a **Foreign Business License (FBL)** is granted (difficult for early-stage startups)

**2. BOI-Promoted Company:**
- 100% foreign ownership allowed
- Tax holidays and work permit exemptions
- Can hire foreign staff without the 4:1 Thai-to-foreigner ratio
- Requires a strong business plan and approval process (can take 2–3 months)

BOI promotion is ideal, but it is time-consuming and not guaranteed for startups. For most SMART Visa applicants, **standard incorporation with a Thai partner is faster**.

---

### Do You Need a Lawyer?

Technically, you can incorporate a company without legal counsel, especially if you’re fluent in Thai. But for foreign founders, using a **Thai corporate law firm or incorporation service** is highly advisable.

Common services offered include:
- Drafting company documents in Thai and English
- Coordinating with DBD and Revenue Department
- Preparing shareholder agreements
- Assisting with banking and tax registration
- Ensuring visa/immigration alignment with business setup

Cost ranges from **20,000 to 60,000 THB**, depending on complexity.

---

### Timeline and Visa Sequencing

Here’s how incorporation typically aligns with the SMART Visa process:

1. **Register Thai company first**
2. **Prepare and submit SMART Visa application**
3. **Receive qualification endorsement (~30 working days)**
4. **Schedule immigration appointment for visa affixation**

Since the visa is tied to your Thai company, incorporation must be completed **before** applying. Expect a minimum of 4–6 weeks to go from name reservation to full visa affixation if no delays occur.

---

### Summary

Setting up a Thai company is a prerequisite for the SMART Visa “S” and requires careful planning, clear documentation, and local cooperation. While BOI promotion offers advantages, most startup founders will proceed via standard incorporation with a trusted Thai partner. Legal support is not mandatory but highly recommended to avoid delays or structural mistakes. With the right preparation, your company can be up and running in under a month—and form the legal base for your SMART Visa journey.

## 7. Building Your SMART Visa Application Package (~1,200 words)

The SMART Visa “S” stream isn’t just a formality—it’s an ambitious government-backed visa designed to attract innovative founders with real technical projects. To secure it, applicants must prepare a full application package that mirrors what one might submit to an accelerator, venture capital fund, or innovation grant program. The bar is higher than many expect.

You’re not just being evaluated for viability. You’re being evaluated for vision, traction, and your startup’s alignment with Thailand’s long-term economic interests. This section breaks the full package into four key deliverables: the business plan, pitch deck, progress report, and a live demo.

---

### a. Business Plan 

**Length and Format:**  
Your business plan should be a structured, clearly written PDF of 8–12 pages. Use clear section headings, charts, and visuals. Avoid fluff. This document should read like a mini feasibility study, and must answer both business and policy-oriented questions.

**Required Sections:**
- **Executive Summary** – concise 1-pager with business overview, traction, and goals
- **Problem and Solution** – articulate the core problem and how your tech solves it
- **Target Market** – size, segmentation, pain points
- **Product Overview** – core features, user flow, screenshots or visuals
- **Technology Stack** – backend, frontend, AI/ML integrations, IP details if any
- **Team** – bios, roles, relevant experience, shareholding structure
- **Business Model** – pricing, revenue channels, go-to-market strategy
- **Financial Projections** – 3-year forecast with revenue, costs, burn, CAC, LTV
- **Impact on Thailand** – why base it in Thailand? Local hiring plans? Economic benefit?
- **Exit Strategy** – acquisition targets, scale plan, long-term vision

**What BOI Looks For:**
- A clearly technology-enabled model with future export value
- Local economic stimulation (hires, partners, skills transfer)
- Long-term vision and potential for global success
- Numerical clarity—include graphs, KPIs, and projected job creation

---

### b. Pitch Deck 

**Format:**  
This is a concise 10–15 slide PDF. Think of it as your narrative pitch—used during review meetings or internal briefings by BOI evaluators. Visual clarity and structure matter more than paragraph text.

**Recommended Slides:**
1. **Cover Slide** – logo, company name, contact info
2. **Problem** – what market gap are you addressing?
3. **Solution** – how does your product solve it? Include screenshots
4. **Market Size** – TAM/SAM/SOM data, with sources
5. **Product Demo** – short flow, GIF or image
6. **Business Model** – how you make money
7. **Technology Stack** – summary architecture, innovation angle
8. **Traction/Progress** – what’s built, early users, test data
9. **Team** – founder bios, org structure
10. **Vision & Exit Strategy** – what success looks like, path to liquidity
11. **Why Thailand** – why not Singapore, Vietnam, or the U.S.?
12. **Ask** – whether seeking funding, partnerships, or just visa approval

**What BOI Evaluates Heavily:**
- Potential for growth in Thailand
- Technology/IP intensity
- Founder capability and clarity of execution
- Specificity: include numbers, timelines, revenue goals
- Alignment with “S-curve” sectors

---

### c. Business Progress Report 

If you’re applying for a 2-year SMART Visa or an extension, you’ll need to submit a **progress report** on your company’s development to date. This is a factual, data-driven snapshot of your work, not a marketing piece.

**Suggested Format:**
- **Cover Page** – company logo, founder name, date, visa type
- **Summary** – 1-paragraph overview of the last 6–12 months
- **Product Milestones** – feature launches, version history
- **Team Growth** – hires, contractors, Thai staff (if any)
- **Partnerships** – Thai vendors, academic partners, pilot customers
- **Traction Metrics** – downloads, DAU/MAU, conversion rates, revenue
- **Screenshots or UI Images** – include proof of development

##  Health Insurance Requirements 

To be eligible for the SMART Visa “S”, applicants—and their family members—must provide proof of comprehensive health insurance valid for the entire visa duration.

### A. Minimum Coverage Requirements

- **Coverage amount**: At least 400,000 THB for inpatient care and 40,000 THB for outpatient care
- **Duration**: Must cover the full two-year visa period (or remaining period if renewing)
- **Applicability**: Required for main applicant and each dependent individually

Policies must clearly state:
- Start and end dates
- Coverage limits (IPD/OPD)
- Name of insured and relation to SMART Visa applicant

### B. Thai vs. International Insurance Plans

**Thai Insurance Providers (Accepted Examples):**
- Aetna Thailand
- Luma Health
- Pacific Cross
- Bupa Thailand
- AXA Thailand

These companies offer SMART Visa-compliant packages explicitly for foreigners and can provide Thai-language documentation when needed.

**International Providers:**
- Allianz Worldwide
- Cigna Global
- IMG Global

International insurance is accepted as long as policy documents meet Thai coverage requirements and are translated into English or Thai with clear benefit breakdowns.

### C. Approximate Costs

- **Thai-based plans**: ~18,000–35,000 THB/year for individuals aged 25–40
- **Family plans**: ~50,000–90,000 THB/year depending on coverage and dependents
- **International policies**: $1,000–$2,500 USD annually depending on global scope

Always request a digital PDF copy of your policy and make sure coverage values are in Thai Baht or include an exchange rate summary.

Failure to present valid health insurance is a common cause of SMART Visa delays—secure this document early.

## 10. Accelerators, Endorsement Agencies, and Government Programs (~500 words)

Although the SMART Visa “S” no longer formally requires a startup to be endorsed by a Thai government agency as of 2025, association with recognized programs, accelerators, or endorsement bodies can still significantly improve your chances of approval. These relationships help validate your business model, signal credibility to reviewers at BOI, and demonstrate that you are contributing to Thailand’s innovation agenda.

### A. Key Endorsement Agencies

Even if no formal endorsement is required, these agencies are involved in ecosystem development and may play advisory or indirect roles in your application.

**1. National Innovation Agency (NIA)**  
The NIA supports innovation-driven enterprises and has programs for both Thai and foreign startups. Participating in an NIA-sponsored event, camp, or grant program (e.g. Startup Thailand League, DEEP Tech Incubators) adds legitimacy to your business. The NIA also helps shape BOI’s perspective on what counts as a “targeted industry.”

**2. Digital Economy Promotion Agency (DEPA)**  
DEPA focuses on promoting digital transformation and is particularly relevant for SaaS, platform, and AI startups. It frequently collaborates with Google, AWS, and local VCs. Attending DEPA webinars, applying for small grants, or building relationships with their regional offices can strengthen your documentation trail.

### B. Thai Startup Ecosystem Anchors

**3. True Digital Park (TDPK)**  
TDPK is one of Thailand’s leading innovation campuses, home to Google Thailand and multiple startup programs. They offer free events, co-working memberships, and access to partners like AIS, Krungsri, and Tencent. Participating in the **TDPK Startup Booster Program** can offer visibility to reviewers and introduce you to government-aligned accelerators.

**4. Startup Thailand / Startup Nation**  
While this initiative is largely branding for the government’s pro-startup policy, being involved in any formal “Startup Thailand” events (demo days, hackathons, conferences) gives your company searchable public exposure—something BOI agents look for during background checks.

### C. Do You *Need* to Be in a Program?

**Short answer: No.**  
You no longer need to be enrolled in an accelerator or endorsed by DEPA or NIA to qualify for the SMART Visa “S.”

**However:**
- Having a grant, award, or partner from one of these agencies helps with the credibility of your pitch deck and business plan.
- Your company is more likely to be perceived as innovative or high-potential if it has touched any layer of the Thai startup ecosystem.
- These programs can also help refine your product, provide local mentors, and connect you with Thai co-founders or legal advisors.

### D. Recommendation

If your startup is new to Thailand and you haven’t participated in any local ecosystem events, apply to at least one government-affiliated program before submitting your SMART Visa application. Even a certificate of participation or accelerator acceptance letter can make a difference in how your application is assessed by BOI.

## 12. Renewing or Extending Your SMART Visa (~300 words)

SMART Visa “S” holders are eligible to renew their visa for an additional two years, but renewal is not automatic. Founders must show tangible startup progress and requalify under the original program criteria.

### Timeline
Start the renewal process at least 60 days before your visa expiry. Delays in endorsement or paperwork processing can jeopardize your legal status.

### Required Documents
- Updated **Business Progress Report** (with KPIs, traction, partnerships, etc.)
- Renewed **health insurance certificate**
- Updated **bank balance confirmation** (600,000 THB minimum held for 3+ months)
- Proof of **active operations**: contracts, invoices, employee payroll, etc.
- Optional: updated pitch deck, new investor letters, product improvements

### Re-Endorsement
The BOI or relevant agency (e.g. NIA, DEPA) will re-evaluate your startup’s performance and potential. You must demonstrate that the business is active, growing, and aligned with targeted sectors.

### Cost
Renewal costs 10,000 THB per year, identical to the original application. Renewals can be submitted through the SMART Visa portal and processed via the One-Stop Service Center or EEC Labour Administration Center.

## Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Rejection

SMART Visa applications are often rejected due to misunderstandings or weak submissions. Here are the most frequent issues:

### 1. Vague or Generic Business Plan
BOI reviewers are not impressed by generic PDF templates or business plans without hard numbers. Avoid fluff—focus on:
- Specific Thai market benefits
- Employment projections
- Exit potential
- Competitive moat and IP

### 2. No Local Entity
If you haven’t registered a Thai company, your application will be dismissed. You must incorporate first and present proof (DBD registration, shareholder list, etc.).

### 3. Weak Demo or No MVP
A working demo is essential. Static mockups or Figma-only prototypes are insufficient. Ideal:
- Publicly accessible web app or mobile app (on iOS/Play store)
- Login credentials for the review committee
- Working backend or sandboxed prototype

### 4. Financial Requirements Not Met
You must show 600,000 THB in personal savings (3-month history) and 180,000 THB per dependent. BOI checks for consistency. Sudden transfers from third parties may trigger rejection.

### 5. Health Insurance Gaps
Coverage must span the full 2-year visa. Use approved providers or show internationally valid plans that clearly indicate the timeframe and region. Missing this is a common technical rejection.

### Pro Tip
Attach an executive summary to your application PDF bundle that clearly outlines all submitted documents, URLs, and relevant login info. BOI reviewers appreciate clarity and structure.

## 14. Final Thoughts – Should You Pursue the SMART Visa “S”? (~400 words)

The SMART Visa “S” isn’t for everyone—but for the right type of founder, it can be a powerful tool.

### Pros
- **Legitimacy:** Gives you legal, long-term residency tied directly to your startup without needing a work permit.
- **Work Flexibility:** No restrictions on job scope or title within your own tech-enabled business.
- **Family Inclusion:** Spouse and dependents can join you with simplified work permissions and reporting requirements.
- **Cost-effective runway:** Operate in Thailand with low burn while maintaining a global tech stack and team.

### Cons
- **Limited global leverage:** This visa doesn’t grant ASEAN-wide mobility or open doors to global VC networks.
- **Application complexity:** You’ll need a strong package—plan, deck, demo, and financial documents—in Thai government-friendly format.
- **Rigid eligibility:** You must register a local entity, meet cash balance thresholds, and maintain clear business progress.

### Ideal Founder Profile
- First-time or second-time founder with a working prototype and product vision.
- Comfortable bootstrapping or operating on limited external funding.
- Interested in leveraging Thailand’s affordability, talent base, and quality of life without relying on it as a funding hub.

### When to Apply
- **Apply now** if you already have a working MVP and are ready to incorporate locally.
- **Wait** if you're still pre-product, haven't formed a team, or aren’t sure about Thailand as your long-term base.

### Long-Term Positioning
The Thai startup ecosystem is maturing, but it still lacks consistent access to early-stage capital, global mentors, and deep AI infrastructure. Treat the SMART Visa not as a launchpad to international scale, but as a stable **runway extension tool**—ideal for focused product development in a low-overhead environment.

### Recommendation
Use the SMART Visa “S” to gain time, focus, and legal certainty. Build your core tech here. Raise funding elsewhere. Thailand can be your base—but don’t expect it to be your full-stack solution.
